Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 27, 2026 · Last Updated: June 27, 2026
🔒 The Short Version
We don't store your data. When you run an analysis, your file is sent over an encrypted connection to our server, analyzed, and the result is returned to you. It is held in memory only long enough to compute your result, then discarded — never written to a database, never used to train any model. The optional "Explain this result" AI feature sends a computed summary (not your raw data) to Google's Gemini API; details below.

1. Who We Are

StatMind ("we," "us," or "our") is a browser-based process statistics platform built for quality engineers. We operate at statmind.tech.

2. How Your Data Is Handled

When you upload a file (CSV, Excel, CMM export) and run an analysis, the data is sent to our backend to compute the statistical result. This processing is:

  • Not stored. Your uploaded data is held in memory only for as long as it takes to compute your result, then discarded. It is not written to a database or saved to disk in normal operation.
  • Not logged. If an error occurs, we log only the error type and request path — never your data, and never an error message that might contain a value from your file.
  • Not used for training. We do not use your data to train or improve any model we operate.
  • Encrypted in transit. All communication uses HTTPS/TLS, with HSTS enforced.

3. AI Explanations — What Is Sent, and to Whom

StatMind offers an optional "Explain this result" feature on analyses. When you click it, we send a computed summary of that result — the statistics and verdict our engine already calculated (for example: Cpk, control limits, p-values, pass/fail verdict) — to Google's Gemini API, which generates the plain-language explanation.

  • We send the computed summary, not your raw data. Your individual measurements and uploaded file contents are not sent to Google — only the scalar results StatMind already computed, and only when you click "Explain this result."
  • The AI explains; it does not decide. The explanation is generated from numbers our verified statistical engine already computed. The AI is instructed to explain only those numbers, not to recalculate or override them.
  • Google's terms apply. The explanation is produced by Google's Gemini API and is subject to Google's API data-use terms. We currently use the free Gemini tier, under which Google may use API inputs to improve their services. If this matters for your data, simply don't use the "Explain this result" feature — every analysis works fully without it.
  • It's optional. Explanations are never generated automatically; you choose to request each one.

4. Information We Collect Automatically

Like most web services, our hosting infrastructure may collect:

  • IP address and general geographic region (country-level)
  • Browser type and operating system
  • Pages visited and timestamps (access logs)
  • HTTP referrer headers

This information is used solely for operating, monitoring, and improving the service. It is not sold or shared with third parties for marketing purposes.

5. Accounts

If you create an account, we store your account details (such as email) to operate the service. We do not associate your uploaded analysis data with your account, because we do not retain uploaded data.

6. Cookies and Local Storage

StatMind does not use tracking cookies or third-party advertising cookies. We use browser storage and authentication cookies only as needed to keep you signed in and to preserve your analysis state within a session.

7. No Third-Party Behavioral Analytics

We do not embed Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, Mixpanel, or similar behavioral tracking tools. Your usage of StatMind is not profiled or monetized through advertising.

8. Regulated Industry Users

We understand many of our users work in regulated environments (medical device, pharma, automotive, aerospace, and similar). Please note:

  • StatMind is not currently validated under 21 CFR Part 11 or IEC 62304, and this page is not a compliance certification.
  • The optional AI explanation feature sends computed results to a third-party API (Google Gemini). If your requirements prohibit sending data to third-party services, do not use that feature.
  • For specific regulatory, validation, or data-residency requirements, contact us and we will tell you honestly what we currently do and do not support.

9. Data Retention

We do not retain your uploaded measurement data. Access logs (IP, timestamps) are retained for a limited period for security monitoring, then deleted.

10. Security

We use HTTPS/TLS for all connections (with HSTS), hardening headers (no-sniff, frame-deny, referrer and permissions policies), input validation on uploads, and error handling designed so failures never echo your data back to you or into our logs. Secrets are stored in the server environment, not in our codebase, and are rotated if ever exposed.

11. Your Rights

Because we collect little personal information beyond standard access logs and optional account details, there is minimal data to request, correct, or delete. If you believe we hold data about you, contact us at the address below and we will respond promptly.

12. Children's Privacy

StatMind is designed for professional use by quality engineers. We do not knowingly collect information from anyone under 16 years of age.

13. Changes to This Policy

If we make material changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date above and, where possible, notify users via in-app notice. Continued use after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
